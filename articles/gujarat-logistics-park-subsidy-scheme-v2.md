# Gujarat Logistics Park Subsidy: A Simple Guide

Gujarat is fast becoming India's logistics hub, thanks to its ports, highways, and industrial corridors. To support this growth, the state government offers subsidies to businesses that build logistics parks, warehouses, and cold storage facilities.

Here's a simple breakdown of who can apply, what you get, and why this sector has strong growth ahead.

> **Note:** Subsidy rates and conditions change periodically. Please confirm exact figures with the latest Gujarat government notification or our advisory team before applying.

---

## What Projects Qualify

- Integrated logistics parks
- Warehouses and distribution centres
- Cold storage / cold chain facilities
- Container freight stations (CFS) and ICDs
- Truck terminals and freight hubs
- Air Cargo Complexes


## Who Can Apply

- New or existing businesses building a logistics facility in Gujarat
- Companies, LLPs, partnerships, Trust or proprietorships registered in India
- Projects on properly zoned industrial/logistics land

## Basic Conditions

- Apply for approval **before** starting construction
- Have valid land use and NA permission
- Get environmental (GPCB) clearance where required
- Operate the facility for a minimum lock-in period
- Start operations within the approved timeline

## Key Benefits Available

- Capital subsidy on eligible investment
- Stamp duty exemption/reimbursement
- Electricity duty exemption
- Interest subsidy on project loans
- Others


## Documents You'll Need

- Business registration, PAN, GST, Udyam (if applicable)
- Land ownership/lease documents and NA permission
- Project report with cost details
- Loan sanction letter (if applicable)
- CA-certified investment proof
- GPCB and fire safety clearances
- Completed application form with project timeline

## How to Apply

1. Get provisional approval before construction
2. Build as per your approved plan
3. Share progress updates if required
4. Apply for final subsidy after operations begin
5. Stay compliant during the lock-in period

## Market Outlook: Why Logistics Parks Are in Demand

India's logistics sector is going through rapid change, and Gujarat is at the centre of it:

- **E-commerce and quick commerce growth** is driving huge demand for organized warehousing near cities and highways.
- **GST and "One Nation One Market"** reforms pushed companies to shift from many small warehouses to fewer, larger, well-located logistics parks.
- **Multi-Modal Logistics Parks (MMLPs)** and the **Dedicated Freight Corridor (DFC)** are improving rail-road connectivity, making Gujarat's logistics parks more attractive.
- **Manufacturing growth (PLI schemes, "China+1" shift)** is increasing demand for industrial warehousing and component storage near ports like Mundra and Kandla.
- **Cold chain demand** is rising fast with growth in food processing, pharma, and organized retail.

Industry reports have consistently shown India's warehousing and logistics real estate segment growing at a healthy pace over the last few years, with **Gujarat, Maharashtra, and NCR** among the top preferred states for large-format logistics parks due to port access, connectivity, and government incentives.


## The Future of Logistics Parks and Warehousing in India

- **Bigger, smarter facilities** – More automation, racking systems, and technology-driven warehouses (Grade A infrastructure) replacing traditional godowns.
- **Green warehousing** – Solar rooftops, energy-efficient buildings, and sustainability becoming a standard expectation, not a bonus.
- **Multi-modal integration** – Warehouses built around rail, road, and port connectivity together, not just highway access.
- **Rise of 3PL and fulfilment centres** – More businesses outsourcing storage and distribution instead of owning it.
- **Tier 2 and Tier 3 expansion** – Logistics parks moving beyond big cities into smaller industrial towns as demand spreads.
- **Policy support continuing** – With India's National Logistics Policy and state-level incentives, government support for this sector is expected to remain strong in the coming years.

For businesses, this means: setting up a logistics park now — while subsidies are active and land costs are still reasonable — can offer a strong long-term advantage.
