Frequently Asked Questions

Do you provide mentorship to early startup or entrepreneurs?

Yes, Subsidy Gyaan offers strategic mentorship and handholding for early-stage startups and entrepreneurs. We go beyond just identifying subsidies—we guide you on structuring your business, optimizing your investment plans, and ensuring compliance to maximize your chances of securing government support and achieving sustainable growth.

Is a bank loan compulsory to avail a government subsidy?

In most government subsidy schemes, yes. Many subsidy programs are credit-linked, which means the project must be financed through a bank or financial institution to become eligible for the subsidy. However, the requirement varies from one scheme to another. Some schemes may allow self-financed projects, while others mandate term loan assistance. Our experts evaluate your project and recommend the most suitable scheme based on your investment, business type, and funding structure.

What documents are required to apply for a government subsidy?
The documentation requirements differ depending on the subsidy scheme, industry, and government department. However, most schemes require certain common documents, such as:
  • Aadhaar Card & PAN Card
  • Udyam Registration (MSME)
  • GST Registration (if applicable)
  • Detailed Project Report (DPR)
  • Bank Sanction Letter / Term Loan Documents (where applicable)
  • Land & Building Documents
  • Machinery Quotations or Invoices
  • Company Registration Documents
  • Financial Statements
  • Other scheme-specific approvals and certificates
Our team provides a customized document checklist based on your project to ensure a smooth and compliant application process.
